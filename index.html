How we work



In Tel Aviv, we develop 6 companies for 6 months.
With projected valuation $100m in 3-5 years,
Reactor invests $100-200k pre-seed round @ ~$1m valuation.

In San Francisco, companies spend 3 months
raising a $2m+ seed round @ $6-8m.
The valuation is set by Bay Area venture capital firm.




What makes us different?


We don't "mentor"

  • Talent

    We believe in finding talent early and overseas. We look for pre-seed high quality teams that can pivot to meet opportunities.

    We invest in people first, space second, idea is subject to pivots and is used to validate the team.

    70% team +25% the-space +5% idea

  • Focused Space

    We focus on opportunities in a hot and productive space - Advanced Internet-Of-Things.

    We believe that smart connected devices are the next big thing for the internet, and were our experience, expertise and contacts can add value to startups.

  • Focused Value

    We focus on preparing the startup for a Bay Area seed round.

    This translates to two major steps. The first is to tune the idea into something the market will respond to.

    The second is traction that earns the seed round from from a Bay Area Venture Capital firm.

Why Tel Aviv?

Tel Aviv startup ecosystem comprise more than 3,000 startups and many accelerators, of these startups about 300 are in the Bay Area, 240 in New York City and 100 in Boston.

(the numbers above are in constant flux)

Tel Aviv is a world class hotbed of innovation without a native market. Startups enjoy the talent and cost benefits of Tel Aviv, but once they achieved a market ready product, startups often relocate to the United States in order to fuel growth by gaining access to deep markets and later stage funding.

The Tel Aviv startup ecosystem is ranked fifth in the world, behind San Francisco, New York City, Los Angeles and Boston. It is third in the Talent index, trailing only San Francisco and Moscow.
We are primarily interested in the Talent Tel Aviv has to offer.

Source: 2015 edition of the Startup Genome Project from Startup Ecosystem Compass

  • Culture

    Strong cultural alignment to the United States

  • Education

    World class technology education

  • Entrepreneurs

    Mature and deep entrepreneurial talent pool

Why San Francisco?

Total Exit volume in 2013, 2014 / The Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2015 by Compass

San Francisco Bay Area startup ecosystem has the most exits by far.
This translates to high valuations for startups regardless of exit type.

In order for a startup to use the global startup ecosystem
efficiently, it should exploits the strengths of each ecosystem
throughout its life cycle:

Work in a smaller, cheaper startup ecosystem when the startup is pre-product market fit. Then move to a larger startup ecosystem after product market fit is reached and it is ready to raise a bigger financing round.

Engineering (pre-seed): Set up an engineering office in a location with inexpensive and plentiful tech talent, such as Tel Aviv.

Productization (seed): Have the executive team headquartered (or at least spend a lot of time) in a well-capitalized location like Silicon Valley.

Market (round A): Depending on the product, set up a sales office close to potential customers and distribution channels, such as New York City.

Internet-Of-Things

TL;DR - Phenomenal growth in Mobile Graphics Processing Technology enables low-power, affordable, client-side Artificial Intelligence and Machine Vision, blurring the lines between Robotics, Virtual Reality and Internet-of-Things applications.

Why Internet-of-Things? Because there is a need for more connected intelligent embedded systems. Internet-Of-Things space is being transformed by Mobile Technology, and there is a lot of value to unlock in the space.

Machine Vision is moving rapidly beyond academic research and factory automation into the world around us. There lots of mature IP to optimize, embed and productize. Realtime performance and network reliability dictated that the processing has to be done in the client, not in the cloud.
