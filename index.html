A Hardware incubator focusing on advanced Internet-Of-Things startups

We take companies from pre-seed in Tel Aviv to a seed round in San Francisco. This means building something impressive enough to raise money. Once we do, we introduce the startup to later stage Bay-Area investors - or even acquirers.

How we work


In Tel Aviv, we develop 6 companies for 6 months.
These companies are selected for projected valuation $100m in 3-5 years.
Reactor invests $100-200k in a pre-seed round @ ~$1m valuation

In San Francisco companies spend 3 months
organizing a $2m+ seed round @ $6-8m valuation
set by Bay Area venture capital firm




What makes us different?

We don't "mentor"

Read more about how we work..

  • Talent

    We believe in finding talent early and overseas. We look for pre-seed high quality teams that can pivot to meet opportunities.

    We invest in people first, space second, idea is subject to pivots and is used to validate the team.

    70% team +25% the-space +5% idea

  • Focused Space

    We focus on opportunities in a hot productive space - Advanced Internet-Of-Things. We believe that smart connected devices are the next big thing for the internet, and were we can add value to startups.

  • Focused Value

    We focus on preparing the startup for a Bay Area seed round.

    We develop the idea and traction goals for the round, we consult on technology and logistics. We help define culture and organization,customer, product and the traction that makes the a seed round possible.

Why Tel Aviv?

Tel Aviv startup ecosystem comprise more than 3,000 startups and many accelerators, of these startups about 300 are in the Bay Area, 240 in New York City and 100 in Boston.

(the numbers above are in constant flux)

Tel Aviv is a world class hotbed of innovation without a native market. Startups enjoy the talent and cost benefits of Tel Aviv, but once they achieved a market ready product, they often relocate to the United States in order to gain access to markets and later stage funding.

The Tel Aviv startup ecosystem is ranked fifth in the world, behind San Francisco, New York City, Los Angeles and Boston. It is third in the Talent index, trailing only San Francisco and Moscow.
We are primarily interested in the Talent Tel Aviv has to offer.

Source: 2015 edition of the Startup Genome Project from Startup Ecosystem Compass

Internet-Of-Things

TL;DR - Phenomenal growth in Mobile Graphics Processing Technology enables low-power, affordable, client-side Artificial Intelligence and Machine Vision, blurring the lines between Robotics, Virtual Reality and Internet-of-Things applications.

Why Internet-of-Things? Because there is a need for more connected intelligent embedded systems. Internet-Of-Things space is being transformed by Mobile Technology, and there is a lot of value to unlock in the space.

Machine Vision is moving rapidly beyond academic research and factory automation into the world around us. There lots of mature IP to optimize, embed and productize. Realtime performance and network realibility dictated that the processing has to be done in the client, not in the cloud.
