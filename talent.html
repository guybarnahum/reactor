We want to be the first investor in the startup. Since hardware startups require capital and hands-on expertise, we blend the best elements of a seed stage firm and incubator. We look for special kind of talent in the team, execution and expertise are key, as is agility and pragmatic approach to solving the market-product fit problem.

We look for teams who listen to the market and can execute for the market.

We believe in finding talent early and overseas. We look for pre-seed high quality teams that can pivot to meet opportunities.

Why Tel Aviv?

Tel Aviv startup ecosystem comprise more than 3,000 startups and many accelerators
Of these about 300 are in the Bay Area, 240 in New York City and 100 in Boston.

(the numbers above are in constant flux)

Tel Aviv is a world class innovation center without a native market. Startups enjoy the talent and cost benefits of Tel Aviv, but once they achieved a market ready product, startups often relocate to the United States in order to fuel growth by gaining access to deep markets and later stage funding.

The Tel Aviv startup ecosystem is ranked fifth in the world, behind San Francisco, New York City, Los Angeles and Boston. It is third in the Talent index, trailing only San Francisco and Moscow.

We are primarily interested in the Talent Tel Aviv has to offer.

Source: 2015 edition of the Startup Genome Project from Startup Ecosystem Compass

    Strong cultural alignment to the United States. Result drivern, pragmatic culture. Access to Asian markets.

  • Education

  • Entrepreneurs

    Mature and deep entrepreneurial talent pool that is aligned to United States Capital.

Why San Francisco?

Total Exit volume in 2013, 2014 / The Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2015 by Compass

Silicon Valley startup ecosystem has the most exits by far. This translates to high valuations for startups regardless of exit type.

In order for a startup to use the global startup ecosystem efficiently, it should exploits the strengths of each ecosystem throughout its life cycle:

Work in a smaller, cheaper startup ecosystem when the startup is pre-product market fit. Then move to a larger startup ecosystem after product market fit is reached and it is ready to raise a bigger financing round.

Engineering (pre-seed): Set up an engineering office in a location with inexpensive and plentiful tech talent, such as Tel Aviv.

Productization (seed): Have the executive team headquartered (or at least spend a lot of time) in a well-capitalized location like Silicon Valley.

Market (round A): Depending on the product, set up a sales office close to potential customers and distribution channels, such as New York City.
