Object Tracking, Detection, Identification and Behavior Analysis

Long Range Biometrics, Behavior Analysis and Physical User Interfaces

In the near future, smart cameras that track and detect moving objects, would be able to perform long range modeling and identification of faces and bodies in “Non-cooperative” environments in realtime.

This kind of breakthrough, next generation visual biometrics, enables novel Internet-Of-Things applications for seamless payment, access control and security, naturally and without the need to explicitly “scan” people.

For example, in retail, customers would be identified upon entry to the store, every item they take would be added to their account on the fly, eliminating the checkout process completely.

Video cameras value today is limited by the need for a person to analyze and act on the feed. Goverments, cities and other high value organizations utilize such capability with great cost. In the near future, low-power, affordible, client-side machine vision is going to eliminate that expensive, unrealible and dull need, opening up the door to many "acting on video" applications. For example smart cameras that are able to detect distress conditions at a home, school or pool, would alert us to emergencies and unsafe conditions. In health care, smart cameras would monitors patient distress and care regimen in hospitals or at home, etc.

These novel Internet-Of-Things applications are a combination of standards, machine vision cloud services as well as compatible hardware that performs large part of the algorithms in the device.
