How We Work

What we do

for startups

We don't "mentor"! We facilitate execution that is tuned to achieve a Bay Area seed round readiness.

Our Philosophy is Focus

TL;DR At Reactor we work with the team to provide the traction needed to show investors, who the startup is, why they should care, and what the startup can do.

The reward is survival : a seed round.

At Reactor, we especially interested in web leveraged IoT / Consumer Technology companies, as we know that our expertise, experience and contacts would add "hard" value to the venture.

We believe that there is nothing more important to a pre-money operation than to get funded. Founders need to get noticed by investors and convince them that their vision will not be ignored by the market. If they do, they still have to prove that they can execute, and this "proof" changes dramatically from investor to investor and from team to team.

We believe in focus, we know that early stage startups have two critical missions: Identity and Survival. Most startups die from indifference by the market and drying of funds. In most cases, at seed, investors are a proxy to the market. And while lack of focus may not kill larger companies, startups will simply run out of money and die.

We focus on preparing the startup for a Bay Area seed round. This translates to two major stages

The first and critical stage is to tune the idea into something the market will respond to. We iterate with the team until we feel we have something great to build. Something that the market would adopt and grow to depend on.

Then we invest. We make sure startups are incorporated properly and are later stage investment ready.

The second stage is defining and achieving the traction that makes the seed round possible. We help founders deal with investors early on, well before the Pitch.

At Reactor we believe, that all of the activities, the money spent and organization in the pre-seed stage are there to serve a clearly defined traction goals and the operational details that follow from these goals. We "pre-flight" the goals (and pitch) with friendly Bay Area Venture Capital investors and our advisors and give feedback to the team. This is done in parallel to the work, so we know that we are building a "investment-ready" company.

These activities include, Minimum Viable Product negotiation, Technology Selection, Hiring and even Logistics. We advise on Company Culture and Organization, Strategic Customer, Social Funding validation, Product development, User Experience and Pitch development.

Once traction has been earned we introduce the startup to the right later stage Bay Area Venture Capital firms. The hard work and careful planning in the pre-seed stage, make's sure that this is not just another low value "cold" Pitch. And when the deal is forming we are there with the startup to support a successful round.

Phases

Concept Development > MVP Planning > Traction                    


                    Investment Planning > Pitch Development > Seed


In the Concept phase, we work with the founders to tune the idea to the market. We define the product, identify the customer and map key traction goals.
Normally the first injection of pre-seed funds happens when the concept is mature enough for Minimum Viable Product development.

In the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Planning phase, we define specific traction goals for the seed round, derive organization and technology architecture, advise on technology selection and skill gap analysis. Basically what to build for seed round and what is needed to build it.
Another injection of pre-seed funds happens when the plan is mature enough to define the amount needed to build the MVP.

We track the team progress in executing the plan, iterating product builds into alpha tests, integrating feedback and changes to meet traction goals and metrics.

Although typical timeframes are 6 months in Tel Aviv for concept, planning and traction, these phases may bleed into each other, or even loop. In parallel, a 3 month period of Investment Planning and Pitch Development are done in San Francisco.

Founders are expected to move to the Bay Area for the Seed round raise. The startup should have a clear location strategy that make sense for investors. Usually startups retain R&D in lower cost, high talent Tel Aviv and establish a San Francisco marketing and funding presence.
