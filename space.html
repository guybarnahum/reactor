Internet-Of-Things

TL;DR - Phenomenal growth in Mobile Graphics Processing Technology enables low-power, affordable, client-side Artificial Intelligence and Machine Vision, blurring the lines between Robotics, Virtual Reality and Internet-of-Things applications.

Why Internet-of-Things? Because there is a need for more connected intelligent embedded systems. Internet-Of-Things space is being transformed by Mobile Technology, and there is a lot of value to unlock in the space.

Machine Vision is moving rapidly beyond academic research and factory automation into the world around us. There lots of mature IP to optimize, embed and productize. Realtime performance and network reliability dictated that the processing has to be done in the client, not in the cloud.

Internet-Of-Things By the Numbers

Hype, Promise, Estimates and Projections

The Promise : Remaking the World

Internet-of-Things promises to remake the world as a heterogeneous collection of "smart" and "connected" things that "talk" to each other and to the internet. This constant chatter that would make our environment aware of itself and of us and that auto configures itself accordingly. It would also generate world "mirroring" data that would increase our insights into it.

This is a vast and ambitous vision. And it is taking place now.

Why now?

While the basic concept Internet of Things is nothing new, several factors came together to push Internet-of-Things over the tipping point.
  • The price of sensors, processors, and networking has come dramatically down
  • Wide deployment of WiFi and Bluetooth, allows for easy to add new networked devices to the home and office
  • Availability of cloud data services simplifies the processing and distribution of device feeds
  • IPv6 allows for almost limitless addressable nodes, far surpassing the number of humans of traditional computers


Why companies invest in Internet-of-Things?

Networking and Computer device companies are looking to add value through sensors enabled services. Intel, Cisco and Network Operators. Internet Companies, such as Google and Amazon try to increase their reach. Incumbents of various verticals and industry try to defend their position through a switch from products to services.
Since Internet-of-Things is seen as a solution for commoditization and competitive pressure by companies, today, there are few verticals or industries that do not invest in Internet-Of-Things projects and the infrastructure it depends on.



Estimates and Projections

At Reactor, we take projections with a grain of salt. Even predicting the past is tricky, and the predicting the future rarly works.
That said, we do belive in the big picture, that Interet-Of-Things is here to stay and that is one of the vectors the internet is progressing on.

According to CISCO projections 2020 would see 50 Billion devices of all kind who are connected to the internet. In 2015 CISCO estimates we have around 18 Billion.

Robotics

The Broadband of Things

Machine Vision > Self Driving, Object Tracking, Detection & Navigation

Revolutionizing Local Deliveries,
Telepresence and Mapping

Amazon, Google and Walmart are waiting for the day when they can deliver goods with flying drones. Meanwhile, a small Estonian robotics startup, Starship Technologies, sees more opportunity on the ground.

In the near future, intelligent and autonomous machines will transport humans, deliver packages and perform various real-world services. In contrast to the hard problems that are presented by self-driving cars, delivery applications have less challenging requirements, slower speeds and more forgiving outcomes.

At the core of the technology is Machine Vision for tracking and detecting moving objects, that would allow these autonomous platforms to perform “detect and avoid” and “follow me” navigation. In addition the capability of analyzing behavior, detecting and recognizing objects, would allow for task event triggering that would let the system respond to traffic lights, elevators and pedestrian crossing and other "physical" interfaces that are part of the environment. Finally, dynamic map construction would update the expected static condition of the map, and allow for route planning and congestion avoidance.

Virtual Reality Video


Streaming & Capturing

3D Scene Capture & Modeling


Google Jump is an exciting new technology that assembles 360 videos that allow you to experience a scene in every direction. Perfect stereo ensures that near things look near, far things look far. The Jump "ASSEMBLER" uses advanced computer vision and a whole lot of computing power to transform 16 pieces of video into stereoscopic Virtual Reality video.

Google has opened the Jump "ASSEMBLER" technology for use by Camera makers. YouTube is already serving such "360 videos".



Telepresence / Virtual Reality Video Streaming

Capturing reality not as flat 2D representation, but as a navigable 3D scene, comprised of 3D “pixels” that faithfully represent reality.

In the near future, this feed would be used to create (render) any desired viewing angle in the scene over the internet, with near real time performance. To overcome the high bandwidth associated with the captured feed, smart caching technology, similar to existing online gaming solutions would be utilized.

Eyes-Of-Things &
Smart Cameras

The internet has Eyes!

Machine Vision >
Object Tracking, Detection, Identification and Behavior Analysis

Long Range Biometrics, Behavior Analysis and Physical User Interfaces

In the near future, smart cameras that track and detect moving objects, would be able to perform long range modeling and identification of faces and bodies in “Non-cooperative” environments in real time.

This kind of breakthrough, next generation visual biometrics, enables novel Internet-Of-Things applications for seamless payment, access control and security, naturally and without the need to explicitly “scan” people.

For example, in retail, customers would be identified upon entry to the store, every item they take would be added to their account on the fly, eliminating the checkout process completely.

Video cameras value today is limited by the need for a person to analyze and act on the feed. Governments, cities and other high value organizations utilize such capability with great cost. In the near future, low-power, affordable, client-side machine vision is going to eliminate that expensive, unreliable and dull need, opening up the door to many "acting on video" applications. For example smart cameras that are able to detect distress conditions at a home, school or pool, would alert us to emergencies and unsafe conditions. In health care, smart cameras would monitors patient distress and care regimen in hospitals or at home, etc.

These novel Internet-Of-Things applications are a combination of standards, machine vision cloud services as well as compatible hardware that performs large part of the algorithms in the device.
